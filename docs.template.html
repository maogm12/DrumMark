DrumMark Guide

A text-first way to write professional drum scores.

Overview

DrumMark is for drummers who already understand meter, grouping, articulations, and standard kit notation.

DrumMark describes drum notation in plain text. Write what you play, and the system compiles it into standard drum notation.

The rendered example below shows a basic rock beat — hi-hat on every eighth note, snare on beats 2 and 4, bass drum on 1 and 3.

Spaces are optional for readability. Tokens like x (hi-hat), d (drum hit), and b (bass drum) map directly to kit pieces. Every token occupies one rhythmic slot, and the slots fill the measure.

  • Comments: everything after # on a line is a comment.
  • Whitespace: spaces and tabs only separate tokens; they do not change rhythm. HH|d-d-| and HH | d - d - | are equivalent.
  • Paragraphs: after the header block, blank lines split layout paragraphs. They only affect system breaks, not musical time.

Headers & Metadata

Defining the technical foundation of your score.

Headers come before any score content and define meter, internal beat grouping, and input resolution.

  • title / subtitle / composer: Text headers for your score's name and credits. Values must be quoted with single or double quotes: title "My Song" or subtitle 'Verse A'. These appear on the printed sheet.
  • tempo: Quarter-note BPM. Default is 120 if omitted.
  • time: The meter (e.g., 4/4, 3/4, 6/8). Required.
  • note: The duration of a single Note Unit (grid slot, e.g., note 1/16). Required.
    • Supported values: 1/1, 1/2, 1/4, 1/8, 1/16, 1/32, 1/64, 1/128.
    • Global: Define it once in the header for the whole score.
    • Overrides: Put note 1/N at the start of any paragraph to temporarily switch resolution (e.g., use 1/32 for a complex fill).
  • divisions: Deprecated Total Note Units per measure. Replaced by note.
  • grouping: Defines the beat structure within a measure. Controls rhythmic grouping and beaming.
    Time SignatureInferred Grouping
    2/4, 2/21+1
    3/4, 3/81+1+1
    4/42+2
    6/83+3
    9/83+3+3
    12/83+3+3+3

    For irregular meters like 5/8 or 7/8, grouping must be specified explicitly (for example grouping 3+2).

  • Syntax form: headers use field value, not JSON and not comma-separated data. Both time 4/4 and note 1/16 are valid.
  • Grouping rule: the explicit grouping parts must add up exactly to the meter numerator. For example, 7/8 may use grouping 3+2+2.
  • Dynamic Grid: the total Note Units per measure is MeasureDuration / NoteValue. In 4/4 time, note 1/16 yields 16 Note Units; in 6/8 time, it yields 12 Note Units.

  • Anonymous tracks: start directly with a barline, such as | x - s - x - s |. They are useful for drum-tab-style fast entry.
  • Explicit summoning: use Track:token to force a single event onto a specific track, such as SD:d, BD:d, RC:d:bell, or ST:R.

Stem direction (voice): Drum notation uses two voices per staff. Voice 1 (stems up) covers hands — hi-hat, ride, crash, snare, toms. Voice 2 (stems down) covers feet — bass drum, hi-hat pedal. The system assigns this automatically based on which track you write to.

Modifiers & Techniques

Syntax: <token>:<modifier> or Track:d:<modifier>. Modifier legality follows the final resolved physical track, not the line where the token was typed. You can chain legal combinations such as SD:d:rim:accent or RC:d:bell:accent.

  • Hi-hat: HH | d:open - d:close - d:ghost - d:dead - |
  • Ride / Cymbals: RC | d:bell - d:drag - d:roll - - | and C | d:choke:accent - - - - - - - |
  • Snare: SD | d:accent - d:rim - d:cross - d:flam - |
  • Toms: T1 | d:ghost - d:drag - d:roll - d:dead - |
  • Bass drum: BD | d:roll - d:dead - d - d |

Rhythmic Groups

Tuplets and custom rhythmic subdivisions.

Groups express rhythmic compression and expansion that do not sit on the regular grid, such as tuplets, stretched dyads, or "two notes across three slots" patterns.

  • Base syntax: [span: item1 item2 ...]. When span is omitted, [a b c] is shorthand for [1: a b c].
  • Meaning: span represents the number of Note Units (slots) the group occupies. Each item inside the group gets duration = NoteValue × span / itemCount.
  • Expansion groups: when itemCount ≤ span, the group stretches note values. It is only legal when the result can land on standard note values, dotted values, or valid tuplets.
  • Compression groups: when itemCount > span, ratios like 2:1 and 4:1 collapse into ordinary subdivision, while other ratios are treated as tuplets.
  • Unsupported cases: any group that would only work if the system had to invent ties is rejected.
  • Nesting: groups may contain groups, but extremely small values below 64th-note territory are not guaranteed to be well supported.
  • [d d d]: the shortest omitted-span form, equivalent to [1: d d d].
  • [3: d d d]: three items across three slots; useful when you want to state the span explicitly.
  • [2: d d d]: three items compressed into two slots.
  • [3: d d]: two items stretched across three slots.
  • [5: d d]: two items across five slots; legal only if the resulting notation remains representable.
  • [2: d d d d]: four items across two slots; treated as ordinary subdivision rather than a tuplet.
  • [3: d [2: d d d] d]: nested grouping for more complex internal subdivision.

Combined Hits

Playing multiple parts simultaneously.

Combined hits use +. Every component starts on the same attack point and shares the same duration.

x+s is the common hat-plus-snare case, and x+s+b means three simultaneous voices. Explicit forms such as HH:d + SD:d are also valid. Spaces around + are optional.

Each component can carry its own modifiers: s:flam + b is a flam on snare with a normal bass drum hit. A group with trailing modifiers applies them to all components: [2: s+b]:accent adds an accent to both snare and bass drum.

Sticking

Hand assignments for complex patterns.

The ST track is mainly for saying how something is played, not what is played. It is especially useful in exercises, teaching material, rudiment assignments, jazz ride/snare orchestrations, and any passage where sticking must be explicit.

Write R for right hand and L for left hand. The annotation attaches to the sounding notes at the same attack point; if an ST position has no real note underneath it, that sticking mark is ignored in export.

The ST track is purely annotation — it does not produce notes or count toward measure duration. It exists in parallel with the musical tracks and does not affect rhythmic validation.

Repeats & Jumps

Structural navigation and alternative endings.

Structural markings are score-global, not private to one track. You may write them on any track line, but they apply to the shared global measure.

Barlines

  • | — normal barline
  • |: — repeat start
  • :| — repeat end
  • || — double barline (no measure between)
  • | | — double barline with an empty measure between (whitespace matters)
  • The score automatically ends with a final barline — no extra syntax needed.

Voltas (Alternative Endings)

  • |1. — first ending; |1,2. — shared by 1st and 2nd repetition.
  • A volta ends at a new volta, at :|, or at |. (explicit termination).
  • Both :| and voltas can span multiple paragraphs — paragraph breaks only affect system layout, not musical structure.

Measure Repeats

  • % — repeat the previous measure; %% — repeat the previous two measures.
  • Measure-repeat bars resolve recursively — if the previous bar is also a repeat, it resolves its content first.
  • % and %% must occupy the whole measure alone. Other tracks on the same bar must stay empty.

Navigation Markers & Jumps

  • Markers: @segno (segno symbol), @coda (coda symbol), @fine (Fine).
  • Jumps: @dc, @ds, @to-coda, and their al-fine/al-coda variants. Legacy @da-capo and @dal-segno are rejected.
  • Placement: @segno at start or interior (not end); @coda at start only; @fine and jumps at end only; @to-coda interior or end (not start).
  • At most one start-side marker and one end-side jump per measure. Nested or crossing repeats are not allowed.

Multi-Measure Rests

Standard notation for extended periods of silence.

Multi-measure rests only accept the form |--N--|, for example |--8--|. There must be at least one - on each side of the number, and the whole structure must occupy a single measure.

Inline Repeats

Quickly Duplicate measures in place.

*N at the end of a measure expands the current measure into N total consecutive copies, not "repeat it N more times." For example, *4 produces four measures total.

Validation

Ensuring musical and logical consistency.

DrumMark validation is primarily about one unique score logic and exact duration closure. The system validates:

  • Whole-measure duration: the exact summed duration of every measure must match the full measure length implied by the meter and note resolution.
  • Grouping boundaries: neither a token nor a whole group may cross a beat boundary defined by grouping.
  • Modifier legality: whether a modifier is allowed depends on the final resolved target track. For example, :open is only legal for hi-hat semantics.
  • Structural conflicts: conflicting repeats, voltas, markers/jumps, illegal groups, and unknown tracks or tokens all fail validation directly.

Common Patterns

A quick-reference cookbook for everyday drum writing.

Here are common drum patterns written in DrumMark. Use these as a starting point or a reminder of the syntax.

Basic Rock Beat

Eighth-note hi-hat, snare on 2 and 4, bass drum on 1 and 3.

16th-Note Groove

Hi-hat plays 16th notes; snare and bass drum add syncopation.

Shuffle / Swing

Use 12/8 time for a triplet-based shuffle feel.

Triplet Fill

Groups create tuplets — three notes in the space of two.

Ghost Note Groove

Use :ghost for quiet, nuanced strokes between the backbeats.

Cross-Stick Verse

Use :cross on snare for a tight, woody attack.

Half-Time Feel

Halve the snare backbeat — snare only on beat 3.

Open Hi-Hat Accents

Use o (open) and :close for barking hi-hat effects.
